Assembling Art

Hello, artist! I’m so excited to introduce two of my favorite artists. They are Robert Rauschenberg and Jasper Johns. Both artists were best- known for their mixed-media artwork.

Robert Rauschenberg was an American painter born in 1925 in Port Arthur, Texas. Rauschenberg didn't begin his career as an artist. In fact, he first went to school to be a pharmacist, but his studies were later interrupted by the war going on at the same time. Once the war ended, he became interested in art and enrolled in art school. Rauschenberg worked with many different art forms, like photography, printmaking, and papermaking, but he was most interested in painting and sculpting. Rauschenberg died in Florida in 2008. He is remembered for his creation of mixed-media artwork.

©

Jasper Johns is an American painter, sculptor, and printmaker born in Georgia on May 15, 1930. Johns spent much of his childhood in South Carolina, where he stayed with various relatives. As a child, Johns's only exposure to art was the paintings his grandmother hung around the house. As an adult, he attended university at the Parsons School of Design in New York City, New York. Today, Johns is best known for his mixed-media paintings of popular symbols, like the flag of the United States.

Both artists were innovative in their use of newspaper clippings, found objects, and paint in their artwork. This technique is closely tied to the concept of assemblage. Assemblage is unrelated materials assembled to create a whole piece of artwork with traditional materials and everyday objects.
