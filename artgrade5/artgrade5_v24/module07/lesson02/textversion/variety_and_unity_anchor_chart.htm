Variety and Unity Anchor Chart

Artists use variety and unity to help create a complete work of art that is pleasing to look at.
Use Variety to maintain interests.

Line – several purple lines – Line size, type, color, and direction add interest.
Shape and Form – several different blue shapes – Shape direction, angle, thickness, and length add interest.

Color – multi-colored shapes – Color adds interest.

Value – image of old pitcher, cup, and apple – Value adds interest.

Use Unity to maintain interest.

Repetition – 3 bugs repeated – Elements that repeat will appear related.

Similarity – a black and red butterfly and ladybug – Elements that are similar will cause the viewer to group things together.

Alignment – 4 snails, 4 grasshoppers, 4 caterpillars – Elements that are lined up will cause the viewer to group things together.
