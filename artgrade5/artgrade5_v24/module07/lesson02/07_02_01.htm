Learn about El Anatsui and his style of art.

El Anatsui 1944-

In February 1944, the sculptor El Anatsui was born in Ghana. He was the youngest child of a very large family. Sadly, at a young age, he lost his mother and was raised by his uncle. One of his first experiences with art was drawing letters on a chalkboard.

©

Anatsui’s work with sculpture and wood carving started as a hobby to keep alive the traditions he grew up with. As a young adult, he attended a top-rated university and later became a professor of sculpture. He taught for over 40 years. At the same time, he developed his own artistic voice and style. He wanted to show that African art could be shown in many ways and not just in a “typical” African art style.

©

Today, Anatsui is well-known for his large-scale sculptures and for transforming trash into treasure. That’s because he doesn’t use traditional materials. Instead, he searches for discarded items such as bottle caps, graters, newspaper printing plates, iron nails, driftwood, and anything he finds fascinating. He takes what others see as junk and uses it to create his masterpieces!

©

Anatsui doesn’t just turn something discarded into something beautiful. He uses objects that are meant to convey a message relating to topics such as consumption, transformation, and the environment. For example, his use of recycled African materials draws attention to the fact that in some places in the world, using recycled material is out of necessity, not choice.

©Unknown, El Anatsui, aluminum and copper wire

These large-scale sculptures are composed of thousands of pieces of trash and bound together with copper wire. They are weighty, shiny, and moveable.

©

By using recycled materials, Anatsui shows how art is for everyone, regardless of how rich or poor they are. Anatsui is one of the most highly acclaimed artists in African history. He is also one of the leading contemporary artists in the world. Anatsui’s art has been on display around the world in places like New York City in the U.S., Barcelona in Spain, Liverpool in the United Kingdom, and Venice in Italy. Anatsui reminds us that “Anytime you touch something, there is bound to be a change.”
