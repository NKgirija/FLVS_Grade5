This large outdoor sculpture called Blue Head is located in Amsterdam, Europe. Camp represents a Nigerian woman wearing a flowing piece of cloth wrapped around her head. The unique headpiece represents her African heritage.

She never felt that she was great at traditional painting, so instead chose to paint metal. She creates royal-looking garments for her sculptures, such as this head wrap. Just like Anatsui, she is using a hard, strong material to represent the soft fabric her subject is wearing in this sculpture. Camp often uses repurposed scrap metal to create her artworks.

El Anatsui and Sokari Douglas Camp shared a commonality in their visually engaging textured artwork with their use of recycled materials and bringing awareness to issues in their community. Take a moment to compare their artwork.

Anatsui cut bottle tops and made them into long strips to create this artwork titled Man’s Cloth. The sculpture's colors are the same as the original bottle caps. Traditional African practices influence his art.

©Man's Creation, El Anatsui, 1998–2001, metal bottleneck wrappers and copper wire

Sokari Douglas Camp created this sculpture with repurposed steel. Her artwork also reflects modern and traditional influences, showing her heritage creatively and skillfully.

©The First Man, Sokari Douglas Camp, 2010, bronze, terrazzo, and stainless steel
