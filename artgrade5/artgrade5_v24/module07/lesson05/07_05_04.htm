Step-By-Step Option 2

It's time to show what you know! Follow these steps to create your Coral Reef.

First, gather the supplies needed for the project. Make sure you review the rubric.

  • White paper
  • Black permanent marker
  • Crayons
  • Texture items (leaves, bark, silverware, etc.)
Coral Reef: Option 2 Steps

Step 1

Research images of a coral reef.

  • Coral reefs have all different shapes, sizes, and colors.
  • Look up some photos to inspire you before you begin.
©

Step 2

Draw a coral reef.

  • Use a black permanent marker to make a contour line drawing of what you’d see when looking at a coral reef.
  • Add a variety of coral, rocks, sea anemones, or sea creatures.

A coral reef is an entire ecosystem! Feel free to draw in sea creatures, shells, and other objects you’d find in a reef.

Step 3

Add color and texture to your drawing with a texture rubbing.

  • Walk around and feel for objects that have texture.
  • Place your drawing on top of the textured object.
  • Use a crayon flat on its side. Rub the crayon over the paper while it’s on top of the textured object.
  • As you color, the texture and pattern should appear.

Try making texture rubbings using real leaves and shells!

Step 4

Add more texture to your drawing.

  • Add more color and texture by hatching, cross-hatching, or stippling.
  • There is no need to make every inch of your paper textured, but you will want to color it completely.

Step 5

Write your name on the bottom of your artwork. Remember to clean up your workspace, put away your supplies, and wash your hands.

  • Remember to clean up your workspace, put away your supplies, and wash your hands.
