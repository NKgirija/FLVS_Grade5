Step-By-Step Option 1

It's time to show what you know! Follow these steps to create your Coral Reef.

First, gather your supplies that are needed for the project. Make sure you review the rubric.

  • Cardboard or paper plate
  • Paint or other coloring materials of your choice
  • Scissors
  • Glue
  • Objects such as: toilet paper rolls, newspaper, sponges, shells, sticks, leaves, sand, cotton balls, cotton swabs, buttons, coins, trinkets, broken toys, etc.
Coral Reef: Option 1 Steps

Step 1

Research and gather materials.

  • Coral reefs have all different shapes, sizes, and colors. Look up some photos to inspire you as you gather materials.
  • Look around your house and outside for items with different shapes and, most importantly, textures.
©

Don’t discount an object just because it doesn’t look like coral. You can always cut, paint, and glue objects together to create your vision.

Step 2

Create the base and plan your composition.

  • Cut a large shape out of cardboard to serve as the base of your artwork. This is where you will glue your other objects.
  • Arrange the objects onto the base until you find a composition that you like best.
  • Don’t glue anything down yet.

The base of your artwork does not have to be rectangular. Get creative!

Step 3

Create coral.

  • Cut and/or combine your found objects to look like items found in a coral reef.
  • Add color to your objects with paint or your material of choice before gluing them down.

Not every piece of your artwork has to represent coral. Consider creating other things you might find in a coral reef.

Step 4

Build a coral reef.

  • Glue down your colorful objects.
  • Consider VARIETY. Spread out larger objects when gluing and fill in the gaps with smaller textured pieces.

Colored, crumpled paper and painted sticks or mulch are great for filling in the space between larger objects.

Step 5

Write your name on the bottom of your artwork. Remember to clean up your workspace, put away your supplies, and wash your hands.

Great job creating your Coral Reef. Your instructor can't wait to see your artwork!
