Practice Time

I like to use implied texture in my drawings because it brings the image to life. It also makes it seem realistic, like I could reach out and feel it.

Get ready to start creating implied textures you can use when creating your own paintings or drawings. In your sketchbook, use a pencil to draw an object and add different implied textures. Think about how you can use the element of art known as line to add implied texture to your artwork.

Once you've finished creating your object with implied texture, show it to a friend or family member. Ask them to provide you with feedback you can use to make your implied textures even better.

©Rosebushes Under the Trees, Gustav Klimt, 1905, oil on canvas

When you think of grass or the leaves on a tree, you might only think of the color green. In fact, artists like Klimt often mix other colors together, like yellow and blue, to create depth and the illusion of texture in their landscapes. These tiny brushstrokes of color work together to create the principle of design known as unity and the element of art known as texture.
