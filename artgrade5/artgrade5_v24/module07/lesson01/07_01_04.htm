Learning how to recreate the actual textures found in nature requires an artist to spend a lot of time practicing. One way an artist can recreate the textures found in nature is by using the element of art known as line. Jump in and explore how to create implied texture with your pencil.

Creating Grass and Shrubs

You can use all sorts of lines to recreate the rough and spikey texture of grass or the uneven texture of a shrub.

©

Creating Leaves

To recreate the texture of a leaf or a feather, you can use diagonal, wavy, curved, or straight lines. You can also choose to use short or long lines.

©

Creating Trees

Recreating the actual texture of trees can be tricky. To draw a tree or wood with a rough, bumpy implied texture, draw many short lines next to each other.
