Elements of Art: Texture

Texture in art is all about feeling.

Something rough or something smooth makes an artwork more appealing.

Painted texture on this forest floor gives you something to explore.

On the branches, there are crunchy leaves, and rough bark covers the trees.

There’s texture in any art you create, like the hard objects on this plate.

This painting is actually smooth, but lines and shapes add texture, too.

The clothes on this mural are not soft at all because they are painted on a rough wall.

We can touch actual texture because it’s real.

How do you think this sculpture would feel?

Each tile on this sculpture looks smooth to the touch, but run your fingers across — they feel bumpy and rough.

Implied texture is when something only looks the way it would feel.

It’s created with lines and techniques — it’s not real.

The clouds in this painting look fluffy and soft, but they are created with brushstrokes and a technique called impasto.

These lines are created with strokes straight and smooth.

Wavy lines create a bumpy texture that moves.

You can create implied texture with lines or with dots by hatching, crosshatching, and stippling lots!

Look at this road — not much texture to see.

Add some stippling, and it looks rough and bumpy.

You can create texture in your artwork, too, with technique and imagination — it’s all up to you!
