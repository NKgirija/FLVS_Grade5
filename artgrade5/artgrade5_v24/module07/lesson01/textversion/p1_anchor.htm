Texture Chart

Texture is how an object feels or looks like it would feel. Texture can bring interest to a work of art.

Actual texture is how something feels. There is a yellow flower, then underneath it says in three-dimensional art, texture is the way something feels.

Implied texture is texture that you can see but cannot touch; a surface that looks like it has texture. There are two pink flowers, showing different angles, and one petal.

Artists use different techniques to create the illusion of texture in two-dimensional art.

Smooth gray sphere - use even shading and value

Hairy - overlap lines and wrap them along the contour of the object

Soft - use even shading and curved lines

Bumpy - use rounded lines inside and around the edge of an object

Rough - use a mixture of dots and short lines

Sharp - use jagged, pointy lines and shapes
