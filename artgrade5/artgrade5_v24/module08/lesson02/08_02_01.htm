Frida Kahlo 1907–1954

©

In July 1907, an artist was born in Mexico City, Mexico. Her name was Frida Kahlo. She had three sisters and lived in a house later called the Blue House, or La Casa Azul. When she was six, Kahlo caught polio, a virus that can cause paralysis. It took her months to recover, and left her disabled.

©

Despite this, Kahlo was a bright student and had great plans to attend medical school. Sadly, she was injured in a bus accident at the age of 18. This event caused her lifelong pain and medical problems. For three months, Kahlo was confined to her bed. It was during that difficult time she returned to her childhood interest and began to paint.

©

A specially made easel enabled her to paint in bed. She had a mirror placed above the easel so she could see herself. During this alone time, Kahlo discovered she enjoyed painting a subject she knew best, herself.

©

Kahlo developed her artistic style using the culture around her for inspiration. She embraced Mexican folk art, which spilled into her paintings. They included bright colors and unique symbols, often mixing realism with fantasy. Painting became a way for her to explore questions of identity and existence.

©

In 1954, Frida Kahlo died unexpectedly at the age of 47. Her work as an artist remained unknown until the late 1970s when her artwork was rediscovered. Today, Frida Kahlo is known around the world for her self-portraits and portraits.
