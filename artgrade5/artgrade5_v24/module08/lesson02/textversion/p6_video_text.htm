Draw with a Grid

When you draw, using a grid is a technique you can use to help you with your proportions.

Proportion is the size of one part compared to another part.

For example, when drawing a face, the forehead is always larger than the nose. They are different proportions.

Let's get proportionate and draw with a grid.

We’re going to practice drawing a face.

We’ll use a grid to guide us.

You can use numbers and letters on the edge of your grid to help you match up your pieces.

See how this piece of the image matches with this piece of the grid? Using a grid lets you focus on one area at a time.

What kinds of lines do you see?

Do you see shape?

As you draw with the grid, you can go back and look at the image you are drawing and make adjustments.

As you move along the grid, try to notice whether any lines continue into the next.

Connect them at the right spot.

As you work, remember to check your progress as you go.
