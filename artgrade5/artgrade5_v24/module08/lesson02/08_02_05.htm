Make a Face

Artists pay careful attention to line and proportion when creating portraits.

Always look at your subject when creating realistic portraits. No two subjects are exactly the same. Artists who create portraits need to spend more time looking at their subjects than keeping their eyes on the paper. Use these tips to create portraits.

©

The facial features you draw for each subject will likely be very different. If you drew two of your friends, they'd likely have very different eyes, mouths, or noses. Even though all facial features are different, it is still good to practice drawing them. Find out how to draw the facial features of a subject.

Drawing Eyes
©

Each subject you sketch will have their own unique eye shape and features. To draw your subject's eyes, you will need to draw their eyebrows, eye shape, and pupil. Adding shading around the eyes is important, too. Practice drawing eyes by using a pencil to complete each of these four steps in your sketchbook.

Drawing Noses
©

Just like each of your subjects will have a unique eye shape, they will also have a unique nose, too. You can use the elements of art known as line and shape to sketch your subject's nose in a two-dimensional artwork. Adding shading around both sides of the nose creates the illusion of a third dimension.

Drawing Mouths
©

Draw a subject’s mouth by following these steps:

  1. Use the element of art, shape, to draw three small oval shapes.
  2. Use line to create the mouth line between the three ovals.
  3. Erase the oval shapes and use line to create the lower lip.
  4. Use shading techniques to create form.
