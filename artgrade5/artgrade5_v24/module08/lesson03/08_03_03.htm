From the Beginning

Last week, my friends and I took pictures in a photo booth. We had so much fun making silly faces for the camera. It made me curious about different kinds of cameras and how they have changed over time. Let’s explore the history of cameras and photography together!

The very first photograph was taken in 1826. It was titled “View from the Window at Le Gras." Photos taken in the 1800s were very different than the type of photography seen today.

© Joseph Nicéphore Niépce Joseph Nicéphore Niépce, 1827, Bridgeman Art Library

In 1888, the first Kodak camera was created. The camera was expensive but easy to use. It became very popular and made photography accessible to the public.

©

In the 1920s, the Leica camera was introduced. This camera used 35mm film, which made cameras smaller and more portable.

©

The Polaroid Land camera was introduced in 1948. This created instant prints. Users no longer had to have their film developed. The camera did it automatically!

©

Digital cameras were invented in the late 1990s. This made it possible for people to preview photos instantly. It also allowed for easy editing and sharing of photos.

©

As smartphones developed in the 21st century, high-quality cameras were built into the phones. This made photography easily available to anyone with a smartphone.

As cameras have changed over time, the style of photographs has also changed. Notice the differences in these photographs.

The first cameras did not take pictures instantly. People had to stay still for several minutes to have their photograph taken. This is why most early photos don’t show people smiling. It is too hard to hold a smile for three minutes or more!
