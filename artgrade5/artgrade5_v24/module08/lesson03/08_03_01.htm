Dorothea Lange

Hey, art friend! I want to introduce another one of my favorite artists, Dorothea Lange. Dorothea was a photographer who used her art to share important information about what was happening in her community.

Dorothea Lange was born on May 26, 1895, in Hoboken, New Jersey. Lange studied photography at Columbia University in New York City. In 1918, she decided to travel around the world, earning money as she went by selling her photographs. Her money ran out by the time she got to San Francisco, so she settled there and obtained a job in a photography studio. During the Great Depression in the 1930s, Lange began photographing the unemployed men who wandered the streets of San Francisco. Her photographs show the desperate condition of these men. Lange’s powerful images would bring the conditions of the rural poor to the public’s attention. Her photographs of migrant workers were often presented with captions featuring the words of the workers themselves. Her passion for capturing people's lives in photographs led her to become a great documentary photographer. Lange continued to take pictures until she died in 1965. However, she is best known for her portraits of displaced farmers during the Great Depression.
