Tips and Techniques

Yesterday, my friends and I were trying to take photos outside of a museum in our community. We took so many different photos. It was hard to get one we really liked, but we didn’t give up!

What makes some photos better than others? When you’re trying to decide if a photo is good, ask yourself:

  • Is it clear or blurry?
  • Can you see everything?
  • Is it interesting to look at?

Say cheese! Here are tips and tricks to help you take great photos.

  • Hold the Camera Steady
  • Focus on Lighting
  • Experiment with Angles
  • Practice and Patience

Photos will look blurry if the camera moves while you are taking the picture. Hold the camera still, as well as what you are photographing. A tripod is a tool to help keep the camera is still.

Video:

Think about where the light is coming from. If you take a photo with the camera facing the sun, it will be very dark. Your pictures will look best if you take photos with the light behind the camera.

Video:

Try taking photos from different angles. Take a photo from above, below, or the side. The photo will look different from each angle.

Video:

Photography gets better with practice. Be patient, and don’t be afraid to try new things and learn as you go. Have fun!
